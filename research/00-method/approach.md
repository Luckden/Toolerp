# Research Approach

This study separates enterprise concepts into five layers of analysis:

- conceptual claims
- operational behavior
- governance relationships
- ownership and funding relationships
- technical dependency relationships

Method rules:

- Do not assume parent-child meaning unless the source explicitly says so.
- Distinguish framework, method, standard, platform, tool, and convention.
- Preserve competing interpretations when the literature or vendors disagree.
- Model relationship types as graph edges rather than flattening them into a tree.

Working hypothesis:

- The enterprise is not a single hierarchy.
- The enterprise is a hybrid of layered systems, dependency graphs, and bounded hierarchies.
- Vendor platforms often compress multiple conceptual layers into one UI or product boundary.
