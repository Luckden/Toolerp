# Operating Models, Capabilities, and Value Streams

Observed pattern:

- The operating model describes how the enterprise delivers value repeatedly.
- Business capabilities are stable abilities that the organization needs regardless of staffing or implementation.
- Value streams trace work from idea, demand, or intent to customer or stakeholder outcome.

Provisional claim:

- Capabilities are a better top-level structural primitive than departments.
- Value streams are a better flow primitive than functions.

Graph model:

- capability -> enables -> value_stream
- operating_model -> governs -> capability allocation and execution patterns
- value_stream -> depends_on -> multiple capabilities

- Strongly aligned with business architecture and EA practice.
